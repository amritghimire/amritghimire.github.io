

Effective budgeting is the foundation of financial discipline. It doesn't have to be complicated or restrictive—it should simply help you understand where your money goes and alert you when spending patterns change. By analyzing your expenses and income over time, you can identify how your finances are distributed across different categories. Finrup's budgeting features make this process intuitive and actionable.

## Why Budgeting Matters

Before diving into the features, let's understand why budgeting is essential:

- **Financial awareness**: See exactly where your money goes each month
- **Spending control**: Identify areas where you're overspending
- **Goal achievement**: Allocate funds toward your financial objectives
- **Trend detection**: Spot changes in spending patterns before they become problems
- **Informed decisions**: Make budget adjustments based on actual data, not guesswork

**Flexible Time Periods**:

## Settings: Create and Manage Your Budgets

The **Settings** section is where you take control of your financial planning by creating and managing budgets.

### Creating Budgets

**Flexible Budget Structure**:
- Create budgets for individual categories
- Set budgets per currency (essential for multi-currency users)
- Adjust budgets monthly or keep them consistent

**Informed Budget Allocation**:
- See **amount used so far** in the selected month before setting a budget
- Review historical spending to set realistic limits
- Avoid the common mistake of setting budgets too low or too high

**Multi-Currency Budgeting**:
- Set separate budgets for each currency
- Perfect for expats, digital nomads, or international businesses
- Track spending limits across different monetary systems

**How It Helps**: Instead of guessing how much to allocate for groceries or entertainment, you can see your actual spending patterns first. This data-driven approach prevents the frustration of budgets that are too restrictive or too lenient.

### Budget Management Best Practices

1. **Review Before Setting**: Check your current month's spending before creating a new budget
2. **Start Conservative**: Set budgets slightly below your average spending to encourage savings
3. **Adjust Regularly**: Update budgets as your financial situation changes
4. **Currency-Specific**: Remember that budgets are currency-specific—set appropriate limits for each

## Suggested Budget: AI-Powered Recommendations

One of Finrup's most powerful features is the **Suggested Budget** functionality, which uses your transaction history to provide intelligent budget recommendations.

### How It Works

**Transaction Analysis**:
- Finrup analyzes your historical transactions
- Identifies spending patterns and trends across categories
- Calculates average spending for each category over time

**Trend Detection**:
- Recognizes recurring expenses
- Accounts for seasonal variations
- Identifies stable spending patterns vs. irregular expenses

**Smart Suggestions**:
- Provides suggested budget amounts based on your actual spending
- Only appears when you have enough transaction history to establish trends
- Updates as you add more transactions

### When Suggestions Appear

The suggested budget feature activates when:
- You have sufficient transaction history (typically 2-3 months of data)
- There are clear spending patterns in a category
- The system can identify reliable trends

**How It Helps**: Setting budgets can be overwhelming, especially when you're new to budgeting or have complex financial situations. Suggested budgets eliminate the guesswork by using your actual spending data to recommend realistic limits. This feature is particularly valuable for:
- First-time budgeters who don't know where to start
- People with irregular income who need flexible budgets
- Users tracking multiple categories who want quick setup
- Anyone who wants data-driven budget recommendations

### Making the Most of Suggested Budgets

1. **Review Suggestions**: Don't blindly accept—review each suggestion against your financial goals
2. **Adjust as Needed**: Use suggestions as a starting point, then adjust based on your objectives
3. **Regular Updates**: As you add more transactions, suggestions become more accurate
4. **Goal Alignment**: Compare suggestions to your financial goals—if dining out is suggested at $500/month but you want to save more, set it lower

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How many budgets can I create?**
A: You can create unlimited budgets for any category and currency combination.

**Q: Do budgets roll over to the next month?**
A: Budgets reset monthly, but you can review historical spending to set appropriate limits for the next month.

**Q: Can I set different budgets for different accounts?**
A: Budgets are category-based, not account-based. This gives you flexibility to track spending across all accounts for each category.

**Q: What if I don't have enough transaction history for suggestions?**
A: Start by reviewing your Overview section to see current spending patterns, then set budgets manually. Suggestions will appear as you add more transactions.

**Q: Can I export my budget data?**
A: Budget information is included when you export your financial data from Finrup.
